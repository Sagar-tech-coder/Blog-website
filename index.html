Humayun's Tomb in Delhi

Humayun's Tomb, located in Delhi, India, is a masterpiece of Mughal architecture and a UNESCO World Heritage site. Built in the 16th century by Empress Bega Begum for her husband, Emperor Humayun, the tomb is a stunning example of Persian-influenced design. Its symmetrical structure, grand arched entrances, and meticulously landscaped Charbagh (four-part garden) set the stage for the later construction of the Taj Mahal.

Taj Hotel, Mumbai

The Taj Mahal Palace Hotel in Mumbai is an iconic symbol of luxury, history, and heritage in India. Built in 1903 by industrialist Jamshetji Tata, this grand hotel stands opposite the Gateway of India, offering breathtaking views of the Arabian Sea. Its blend of Indo-Saracenic and European architectural styles, along with its legendary service, has made it one of the world's most celebrated hotels.
